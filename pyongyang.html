Same metric. Very different cities.

Cuba has 7x the GDP per capita. Havana has the Hotel Nacional, the Malecon, real charm. But even at its best angle, it is visibly aging. The state cannot maintain what it built decades ago.

Bangladesh has 4x the GDP per capita. Dhaka is growing fast, with real commercial energy. But the built environment is uneven, crowded, improvised.

Pyongyang has the lowest GDP of the three. Last month, it completed 50,000 new apartments in five years, with towers connected by skybridges. The city looks like a rendering. That's not prosperity. That's a command economy concentrating everything into one showcase.

Flood to greenhouses in under two years

The same site, devastated by flooding in 2024, rebuilt with industrial greenhouse facilities by 2026. Beyond Pyongyang, the state built 20,000 rural homes in 2023 and 25,000 more in 2024. This is not a country in free fall. It is a state that can mobilize resources at scale when it decides to.

GDP measures aggregate output. It does not measure what a city looks like, what a state can do, or how dangerous it actually is.
