The poorest capital looks the best

Cuba has 7x the GDP per capita of North Korea. Havana's buildings collapse regularly. Officials estimate 28,000 people live in structures that could fall at any moment.

Bangladesh has 4x the GDP per capita. The Karail slum in Dhaka houses hundreds of thousands in corrugated tin stretching to the horizon.

Pyongyang's GDP is the lowest of the three. In March 2025, it opened its third 10,000-unit housing district in four years, with towers connected by skybridges. That's not prosperity. That's a command economy concentrating everything it has into one showcase.

State capacity

Flood to greenhouses in under two years

The same site, devastated by flooding in 2024, rebuilt with industrial greenhouse facilities by 2026. This is not a country in free fall. It is a state that can mobilize resources at scale when it decides to.

GDP measures aggregate output. It does not measure what a city looks like, what a state can do, or how dangerous it actually is.
